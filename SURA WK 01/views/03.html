Reading 03 - Animation, Simulative Motion, and Magical Realism

Reality vs dreams:

- Animation is worldbuilding

- Has immense potential, to negotiage reality and fantasy as well as using animation to rethink architectural and spatial experiences.

- Reimagine our physical world and speculate on how it could be

- View the concept of reality through lenses of stroytelling and worldbuilding + inspect reality as part of extended reality and a physical virtual spectrum

- Our dreams and imaginations are the closest to the fantasy world

- The in betweens states of real and fantasy, one of the most recognised being the magical real.

- Essentially the beauty in the ambiguity, room for audience imagination, like Chinese philosophical idea of liu bai

- Magical realism can be considered a vehicle, to live and experience without detaching from the everyday world while being fully immesered in fantasy and dreams

- Understanding of 'story'

- Miyazaki wanted to capture the strange and yet familiar; this is like dreams, reminds me of surrealism in artmaking, minus the psychoanalysis aspects

- Ghiblis success is in merging real with unreal, making abnormal events credible by placing it in a familar contect that anchors the magical back into ordinary reality.

- Everything happens in landscapes not far from our own cityscapes, making possibilities, buildings with similar interior intimacy. Example: Howls castle being extremely typical inside, yet its a moving castle in relation to tis urban/rural context. Bathhouse is completely ordinary from the outside and blends into the context of the village, yet its users, operation, and scale all together is completely abnormal.

Animation as Magic:

- Animation is bending and twisting physical/tangible reality.
